Is magic an illusion or a trick?

Magic is fundamentally the art of illusion—combining sleight‑of‑hand, theatrical storytelling, and psychological misdirection to evoke wonder.

In ancient Egypt, magicians like Djedi (around 2700 BCE) performed early cup‑and‑ball illusions—a precursor to entertainment magic.

Jean‑Eugène Robert‑Houdin (1805–1871), the “father of modern magic”, transitioned magic from street fairs to formal theaters, emphasizing elegance and craftsmanship.

Harry Houdini (1874–1926), who took his stage name from Robert‑Houdin, became world‑famous for daring escapes, debunking spiritualists along the way.

David Copperfield (b. 1956) revolutionized stage illusion with grand spectacles like making the Statue of Liberty vanish and walking through the Great Wall of China.
